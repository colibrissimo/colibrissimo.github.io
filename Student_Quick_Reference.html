The 4 Harvard Principles

1
Focus on Interests, Not Positions
Ask "WHY" to discover what people really need. Positions are what they say they want. Interests are why they want it.
üìñ Example: Library window story - one wanted fresh air (interest), one didn't want wind on papers (interest). Opening a different window solved both.
2
Use Fair Standards
Use objective criteria like market prices, expert opinions, or regulations instead of personal demands.
üéÇ Example: Cake cutting - one person cuts, the other chooses first. This ensures fairness.
3
Invent Options for Mutual Gain
Brainstorm creative solutions. Differences aren't problems - they're opportunities for win-win outcomes.
üçä Example: Orange story - one wanted peel for baking, one wanted fruit to eat. Both could have gotten 100%, not just 50%.
4
Separate People from the Problem
Be soft on the person, hard on the problem. Don't let emotions block agreement. Build relationships.

BATNA
Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement

Your BATNA is your "walk away" option. The better your alternatives, the more power you have in negotiation.

üíº Job Offer Example:

No BATNA: You have no other offers ‚Üí Weak position

Strong BATNA: You have 2 other job offers ‚Üí Strong position

Always develop your BATNA before negotiating!

Negotiation Jujitsu

When the other party attacks you or is being difficult, sidestep rather than push back:

1. Look Behind Their Position

Don't just say yes or no. Ask: "Why do you want that?"

2. Ask for Their Advice

"If you were in my position, what would you do?"

3. Invite Criticism

"What's wrong with this proposal? Help me understand your concerns."

In negotiations, asking who's winning is like asking that in a marriage. If you're focused on winning, you've missed the point. The real goal is working together and finding a solution that satisfies both sides' interests.

üìù Practice Scenarios

Scenario 1: Salary Negotiation

Your Position: I want $60,000

Better - Focus on Interests: "I'm looking for a salary that reflects my 5 years of experience and the market rate for this role. Can we discuss what's fair based on industry standards?"

Scenario 2: Project Deadline

Your Position: I need 2 more weeks

Better - Focus on Interests: "The deadline is important. My concern is delivering quality work. What if we prioritize the most critical features for the deadline and schedule the rest for phase 2?"

Scenario 3: Office Space

Your Position: I want the corner office

Better - Focus on Interests: "I need a quiet space for client calls and natural light for video conferences. Let's look at all options that meet these needs."

Remember: Win-Win is Always Possible!
